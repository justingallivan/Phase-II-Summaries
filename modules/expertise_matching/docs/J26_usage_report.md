### 4. Research Program Staff — Expertise & Load Assessment

> Note on expertise summaries: All Expertise fields in the reviewer database are written as domain-level summaries — no paper citations, named outputs, lab names, or specific publication references.

**Jean Kim — 65 proposals (89% MR)**

Kim is the program's primary MR reviewer. Her portfolio is anchored in iPSC disease modeling, neurodegenerative disease, neuronal molecular biology, stem cell aging, cancer genomics, and immune-cell biology. The 65-proposal load reflects genuine expertise concentration rather than administrative default — the MR program skews heavily toward her domains. Residual gap: the 6 Alzheimer's disease proposals are handled at the methodology level by Kim via iPSC platform expertise, but the AD-specific molecular biology (tau propagation, amyloid processing, microglial activation) requires the TBD neurodegeneration specialist consultant.

**Justin Gallivan — 63 proposals (61% SE, 38% MR)**

Gallivan carries the most balanced SE/MR split on the team. His chemistry background is biochemistry and synthetic biology — riboswitches, directed evolution, microbiome engineering — not synthetic organic or materials chemistry, which is why condensed matter physics and polymer mechanics proposals sit with Pruitt. His MR load is primarily RNA biology, functional genomics, and cell biology proposals with strong molecular methods components. Load is well-calibrated; no redistribution warranted.

**Kevin Moses — 55 proposals (50% SE, 49% MR)**

Moses has the most balanced program split, reflecting his role as the team's broadest scientific evaluator. His Drosophila genetics and conserved signaling expertise (Wnt/Wingless, Hedgehog, Notch, EGF/MAPK) translates well across developmental biology, regenerative biology, and neuroscience proposals in both programs. His load is 10 below Kim's because the MR program overrepresents iPSC and neurodegeneration content that is squarely Kim's domain.

**Beth L. Pruitt — 47 proposals (95% SE)**

Pruitt's 47-proposal primary load is irreducible. She is the only staff member who can evaluate SE physics and instrumentation proposals — condensed matter physics, NEMS/MEMS, cryogenic transport, nanofabrication, quantum sensing, soft matter mechanics, and optical instrumentation. Moses has no condensed matter physics background; Gallivan's chemistry does not extend to materials physics or polymer mechanics. Four proposals where Pruitt was the strongest scientific fit were reassigned to Moses or Gallivan as primary lead, with Pruitt in a secondary role (marked with an asterisk) to be looped in on review. She also backs up the lead PD on an additional 31 proposals where her instrumentation or biophysics expertise is relevant.

### 5. Board Members — Individual Profiles & Proposal Engagement

**William R. Brody — 12 board interest proposals**

Brody's engagement profile is the broadest of any board member, and his scientific standing is deeper than his administrative titles suggest. His original research established foundational methods in medical imaging: Doppler ultrasound flowmetry, dual-energy X-ray subtraction imaging (sole-inventor US patent), intravenous digital subtraction angiography, and gated cardiac CT. His 12-proposal interest list covers a wide range of imaging modalities — super-resolution and FLIM microscopy, AI-guided deep-tissue optics, single-axon brain connectivity mapping, advanced cardiac and liver imaging — and he can evaluate both the underlying physics and the translational design of these proposals.

**Kelsey C. Martin — 6 board interest proposals**

Martin is the board's specialist in neuroscience and RNA biology. Her research centers on synaptic plasticity, local mRNA translation at synapses, activity-dependent transcription, synapse-to-nucleus signaling, and epitranscriptomics (m6A modification). Her 6 interest proposals map precisely onto these domains. Her NAS membership and Pasarow Award in Neuropsychiatry reflect standing in this community. She is among the stronger scientific voices on the board for MR neuroscience proposals.

**James S. Economou — 6 board interest + 9 consultant proposals (15 total)**

Economou is the board's specialist in tumor immunology and cancer immunotherapy — CAR-T, TCR-engineered T cells, dendritic cell vaccination, adoptive cell therapy, and the tumor microenvironment. His 6 board interest proposals are in his core domain. Beyond that, he was identified as a consultant-level match on 9 additional MR proposals where immune-cell biology is central. With 15 proposals engaged, he is the most broadly deployed board member in J26.

**Edward M. Stolper — 5 board interest + 6 consultant proposals (11 total)**

Stolper is the board's earth and planetary scientist, with deep expertise in igneous petrology, volatile solubility in silicate melts, experimental mineralogy, and planetary geochemistry. His 5 board interest proposals are squarely in his domain. He was additionally identified as a consultant-level match for 6 SE proposals involving abiotic chemical reaction networks, prebiotic chemistry, and geoscience instrumentation. His Wollaston Medal, Roebling Medal, and NAS membership reflect genuine standing in these fields.

**Kent Kresa — 4 board interest proposals**

Kresa is an aerospace and defense systems engineer. His 4 proposals of interest are those with clear aerospace instrumentation or defense-technology adjacency. His perspective is engineering systems rather than fundamental science — most useful for questions about technical readiness, scalability, and mission design. He does not provide scientific peer review of the underlying physics claims.

**Andrea M. Ghez — 3 board interest proposals**

Ghez is the board's astrophysicist, with a Nobel Prize for demonstrating the existence of a supermassive black hole at the galactic center using adaptive optics on Keck Observatory. Her 3 interest proposals are tightly matched to her observational astrophysics and instrumentation expertise. List is small by design — she can evaluate both the scientific claims and the instrumentation approach in these proposals at a depth no other board member can match.

**Thomas E. Everhart — 2 board interest proposals**

Everhart is a physicist who co-invented the Everhart-Thornley secondary electron detector and developed the foundational theory of voltage contrast imaging of semiconductor junctions. His 2 interest proposals are precisely matched: electron beam instrumentation and cryo-EM detector physics. List is short by design — both are genuine domain matches, not courtesy assignments.

**Robert A. Bradway — 5 proposals (industry perspective only)**

Bradway has deep pattern recognition on what makes biologic drug programs viable at scale — ADC development pipelines, PCSK9 inhibitor deployment, biosimilar competition strategy. His 5 flagged proposals are those where this industry lens genuinely adds something. These should be explicitly solicited as industry/translational perspective input, not scientific peer review.

**Richard N. Foster — 0 proposals**

Foster's perspective is strategic and economic rather than scientific. No proposals in J26 were identified where this lens adds distinct value that staff or other board members cannot provide.
